We are STEM Inc.


We are STEM (WSTEM) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, led by students of color in NYC. Our goal is to bridge the gap between NYC’s underrepresented young adults and the STEM fields. This includes, but is not limited to; inspiring NYC’s younger generation of minorities to pursue STEM by allowing them to explore the various aspects and unfamiliar subjects within it. Networking our members with both students and professionals of color in STEM will allow them to exchange experiences, create stronger bonds as a community, help obtain internships or job offers and witness how people of color play a large role in STEM. We also strongly believe in enhancing people of color’s critical skills and technical skills, that are not taught by every institution, yet are vital for professional career and growth, such as Python, MATLAB, Excel, etc.

Our History

WSTEM was founded in March of 2019 by Rua Hamid while she was only 21 years old as an undergraduate in CUNY, Queens College. Being the only student of color as a Geology major, she faced and noticed many disparities against her in the field. As a result, she assembled a group of students of color in NYC that shared an interest in the sciences and created WSTEM. In February of 2020, WSTEM officially became a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. WSTEM is an obligation to demonstrate and prove that WE, people of color, are needed and belong in the sciences and will not be held back further due to our current social, political and economic environment.
